Case Study

Marketing and Sales Operations
Built from Zero

B2B SaaS  ·  Energy Sector  ·  Full Ops Infrastructure  ·  6 Workstreams

01 — Overview

Project Overview

When joining a growing B2B analytics and technology company in the energy sector, the organisation had strong technical expertise and industry credibility — but lacked the operational infrastructure required to scale marketing, events, sales enablement, and client communications effectively.

The role evolved beyond traditional marketing execution into building the foundational systems, workflows, and processes needed to professionalise go-to-market operations. The result: a fully functioning marketing and sales operations ecosystem enabling repeatable processes, improved cross-functional collaboration, and measurable organisational efficiency.

The Core Challenge

The organisation was in a growth stage but operating with fragmented tools, no centralised workflows, and heavy manual coordination across sales, marketing, and leadership. Scaling commercial activities without operational infrastructure was creating inconsistent client experiences and significant execution drag.

What Was Missing

Process Gaps

  • No centralised event management workflow
  • No formalised RSVP or event registration process
  • Inconsistent proposal and sales collateral processes
  • Limited lifecycle communication strategy
  • Inconsistent brand and design systems

Infrastructure Gaps

  • Limited email marketing infrastructure
  • Fragmented tools with disconnected workflows
  • No sales enablement ownership or governance
  • Heavy manual coordination across all teams
  • No reporting or campaign performance tracking
02 — Strategy and Execution

Six Workstreams Built

The build-out was structured across six interdependent workstreams — each addressing a critical operational gap and designed to function as a cohesive system once complete.

Workstream 01
Event Operations Infrastructure

Events were a major channel for business development and client engagement, but operated without any operational systems. Built a complete end-to-end event operations workflow covering invitation design, registration, confirmation, logistics, and post-event follow-up.

Implemented email marketing infrastructure for segmented invitations, RSVP workflows with calendar integrations, automated confirmation sequences, and ICS file generation for attendee calendar management.

Outcomes
  • Fully automated RSVP and confirmation workflows
  • Standardised templates for every event touchpoint
  • Reduced manual coordination overhead significantly
  • Consistent attendee experience across all events
Workstream 02
Marketing Automation and Lifecycle Comms

Email communications were primarily transactional and tactical. Redesigned lifecycle communication strategy to transition from feature-driven announcements toward value-based engagement that supported product adoption and retention.

Implemented newsletter workflows, segmented audience messaging, campaign templates, and automated nurture sequences — with standardised reporting metrics to measure performance.

Outcomes
  • Scalable lifecycle marketing foundation in place
  • Segmented messaging by audience and engagement tier
  • Insight-driven content replacing transactional announcements
  • Standardised campaign reporting metrics introduced
Workstream 03
Sales Enablement Infrastructure

Sales collateral lacked standardisation, consistency, and operational ownership. Built and standardised the full proposal and collateral ecosystem — from modular proposal templates supporting both domestic and international workflows to one-pagers, presentation templates, and branded sales assets.

Outcomes
  • Faster proposal turnaround through modular templates
  • Improved brand consistency across all sales materials
  • Reduced manual design work on ad hoc requests
  • Stronger sales readiness and presentation quality
Workstream 04
Brand and Design Systems

To support scalable asset production, built a repeatable design ecosystem covering brand-aligned templates, event collateral systems, social media asset workflows, and presentation templates — with internal design standards that reduced dependency on ad hoc requests.

Outcomes
  • Consistent brand expression across all channels
  • Self-serve template library for recurring asset types
  • Reduced bottlenecks on design production
  • Improved quality and speed of collateral delivery
Workstream 05
Digital and Web Operations

To improve agility and reduce reliance on external support, applied front-end development skills directly to marketing operations — building custom landing pages, event microsites, RSVP pages, and database-connected reporting tools.

Applied capabilities in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, CRM querying, and marketing technology administration to reduce deployment bottlenecks and increase execution speed across teams.

Outcomes
  • Custom landing pages deployed without external dependency
  • Event microsites and RSVP pages built in-house
  • CRM reporting and database queries handled internally
  • Faster time-to-launch on digital initiatives
Workstream 06
Commercial Alignment and Cross-Team Ops

Marketing and sales operated with limited process alignment. Introduced shared workflows, standardised collateral governance, repeatable campaign processes, and executive communication support to improve cross-functional execution.

Established social amplification processes for company announcements and campaigns, and built the operational cadences that kept commercial teams coordinated at scale.

Outcomes
  • Shared workflows reduced duplication and handoff friction
  • Standardised collateral governance across sales and marketing
  • Executive communications streamlined and professionalised
  • Consistent external brand voice across all channels
03 — Operational Stack

Infrastructure Operationalised

The operational stack was built and maintained across six functional tool categories — selected and configured to work as an integrated system rather than isolated point solutions.

Email Marketing Automation
Campaign workflows, segmentation, nurture sequences, performance reporting — Mailchimp, Zoho Campaigns
Event Registration and RSVP
End-to-end event workflows, calendar integrations, ICS generation, automated confirmation sequences
Design and Brand Management
Template libraries, brand standards, asset production — Canva, InDesign, brand governance documentation
CRM and Workflow Tools
Contact management, pipeline tracking, reporting, cross-team workflow coordination — Zoho One Suite, Salesforce
Proposal and Document Automation
Modular proposal templates, contract workflows, branded document production — PandaDoc (Certified Admin)
Web and Landing Page Platforms
Custom pages, event microsites, RSVP flows built in HTML/CSS/JavaScript — Wix, custom development
04 — Business Impact

What the Build Enabled

The infrastructure build transformed marketing from a primarily execution-based function into an operational growth engine. The outcomes below reflect what became possible once the foundational systems were in place.

  1. 01

    Scalable event programme built from the ground up

    The event operations infrastructure directly enabled the multi-year programme detailed in the Events and Engagement case study. Without standardised RSVP workflows, confirmation sequences, and attendee management systems, a programme at this scale would not have been operationally viable.

  2. 02

    Commercial growth from zero to a substantial base of major operator accounts

    Standardised proposals, sales collateral, and lifecycle communications created the infrastructure that supported a full commercial pipeline — from prospecting through onboarding and post-sale expansion. The operational backbone made scale achievable.

  3. 03

    96%+ retention rate supported by lifecycle communication systems

    The shift from transactional email to value-based lifecycle communications — product updates, adoption content, insight-driven newsletters — created ongoing client touchpoints that directly supported retention and feature adoption across the user base.

  4. 04

    Reduced operational drag — faster execution across all teams

    Before centralised systems existed, each campaign, event, or proposal required significant manual coordination. Repeatable templates, shared workflows, and automated sequences removed the recurring friction that slowed commercial execution.

  5. 05

    In-house digital capability reduced external dependency

    By applying front-end development skills directly to marketing operations, the organisation avoided the cost and lead time of external agency or contractor support for landing pages, event microsites, and database reporting — increasing agility materially.

The Compounding Effect

Each workstream was individually valuable — but the real impact came from their integration. Event infrastructure fed the CRM. Lifecycle communications used the same segmentation logic as event targeting. Proposal automation used brand assets managed in the design system. The whole was substantially greater than the sum of its parts.

05 — Role Evolution

A Hybrid Function

The scope of this work extended well beyond a conventional marketing remit. The role evolved into a hybrid function spanning six operational domains — each requiring distinct technical, strategic, and executional capability.

Marketing Operations Sales Operations Revenue Enablement Event Operations Marketing Technology Administration Brand Systems Management

This breadth is the defining characteristic of the work. The infrastructure built was not a series of isolated projects — it was a deliberate, integrated operational architecture designed to support growth at scale.
