01 — Overview

Project Overview

This project built a comprehensive event engagement analytics framework for Platform — a B2B software platform serving the upstream oil and gas sector. Spanning 26 events and 4+ years of data (2022–2026), the analysis tracks how 1,556 contacts interact with the platform through in-person quarterly sessions, virtual webinars, and special events.

The objective was to move beyond simple attendance counts and build a layered engagement intelligence system: segmenting contacts by engagement depth, identifying the most active accounts, benchmarking event formats, and uncovering the overlap between event attendees and platform users.

Key Finding

Despite 57% of the contact database being SaaS platform users, only 35.7% have ever attended an event. The overlap between heavy platform users and repeat event attendees — the "Platform Champions" — numbers just 30 people, but they represent the most deeply engaged segment in the entire database.

02 — Methodology

How It Was Built

1
Data Consolidation and Contact Matching

26 individual event attendance records were compiled and matched against a master contact database of 1,556 records. Each contact was cross-referenced with platform user status (Platform User flag), enabling event attendance and platform usage to be analysed in tandem.

2
Engagement Segmentation

Contacts were classified into three engagement tiers based on cumulative event attendance: Dormant (0 events), Casual (1–2 events), and Repeat (4+ events). This segmentation was then cross-tabulated with SaaS platform user status to reveal the engagement quality distribution across the database.

3
Champions and Heavy Users Identification

Two high-value cohorts were identified independently: Highly Engaged contacts (4+ events attended) and Heavy Platform Users (10+ platform hours YTD). The overlap between these two groups — contacts in both — was flagged as the "Platform Champions" segment, representing the most deeply invested users in the database.

4
Event Format and Timing Benchmarking

All 26 events were categorised by format (Quarterly, Webinar, Special), time of day (Morning / Afternoon), and month. Attendance rates, cost-per-attendee, and year-over-year growth were calculated across all dimensions to identify which formats and timing windows perform best.

5
Company-Level Engagement Analysis

For each of the 40 tracked companies, total attendances, average events per person, engagement rate, year-over-year attendance presence, and segment mix (Repeat / Casual / Dormant) were calculated. This enables account-level prioritisation for event outreach and follow-up.

03 — Growth

07 — Implications

What the Data Tells Us

  1. 01

    2025 was the inflection point — and 2026 is proving it wasn't a fluke

    Attendance tripled from 2024 to 2025 (110 → 288 unique attendees) on the back of a doubling of events. The 2026 data — 175 unique attendees across just 4 events YTD — confirms the programme has structurally shifted to a larger audience base, not just a one-year spike.

  2. 02

    Webinars are the programme's most underinvested asset

    Webinars achieve a 3.2% attendance rate at zero cost, versus 2.6% for paid quarterly sessions. The 2025 New Grads webinar (97 attendees) and the 2026 Platform Basics webinar (77 attendees) are two of the top five best-attended individual events in the programme's history. Scaling webinar cadence is the highest-ROI lever available.

  3. 03

    64% of the database has never engaged with an event — a major activation opportunity

    1,001 Dormant contacts represent a significant untapped audience. Crucially, 501 of them are SaaS platform users — already invested in the product but not yet drawn into the event community. A targeted activation campaign for this sub-segment could significantly expand the engaged base without needing to acquire new contacts.

  4. 04

    The 30 Platform Champions are the programme's highest-leverage relationship asset

    These contacts attend events regularly and use the platform heavily. They represent the ideal profile for product champions, peer referrers, case study participants, and beta testers. The programme currently has no formal mechanism to recognise or leverage this group — a gap worth closing.

  5. 05

    Per-capita engagement matters more than raw contact count

    Several large accounts (Operator E with 36 contacts: 0 attendances; Consulting Firm A with 124 contacts: 2.4% rate) punch far below their weight. Meanwhile Operator K (8 contacts, 87.5% rate) and Operator K's 2.38 avg events per person demonstrate that smaller, more invested accounts deliver better community value. Account prioritisation should weight engagement rate alongside size.

08 — Recommendations

Action Priorities

Double the webinar cadence

Webinars outperform quarterly events on attendance rate at zero cost. Increasing from 10 to 15+ webinars per year — particularly using proven topics like Platform Basics and module-specific content — is the highest-ROI programme investment available.

Launch an Platform Champions recognition programme

The 30 contacts in both the Highly Engaged and Heavy Users groups should be formally acknowledged. A named tier, early access to features, or invitation to an advisory circle converts organic advocates into structured brand assets.

Run a targeted activation campaign for dormant platform users

501 contacts are SaaS platform users who have never attended an event. A personalised email campaign — positioned as "you use Platform, come meet the community" — is the lowest-friction activation play in the database.

Shift the event calendar toward Q1, Q2, and June

February, June, and January are the three best-performing months by attendance rate. October and November consistently underperform. Concentrating events in the Jan–June window should lift average attendance rates without requiring additional spend.

Build account engagement scorecards for the top 15 companies

Combining contact count, attendance rate, segment mix, and year-over-year trend into a per-account score enables the sales and events team to prioritise which accounts need activation vs. which are already deeply engaged and ready for expansion conversations.
