01 — Overview

Project Overview

The platform had already established strong positioning in upstream analytics — serving operators, investors, and technical teams with well, production, and geological intelligence. A strategic opportunity emerged to expand into midstream analytics, broadening product capabilities and unlocking a new segment of users focused on facilities, infrastructure, throughput, and operational optimisation.

I supported the commercialisation and go-to-market launch of the Midstream Module — translating a technically complex product expansion into a market-ready offering with clear positioning, launch infrastructure, and sales enablement.

Why This Launch Was Different

This was more than a feature release — it was a platform expansion into an adjacent but operationally distinct product category. It required both deep product storytelling to bridge upstream and midstream workflows, and operational build-out of launch infrastructure that didn't yet exist.

The Core Challenges

Market and Positioning

  • Platform strongly associated with upstream — midstream was a new category
  • Technically complex workflows needed business-oriented translation
  • Messaging had to fit within an established platform narrative
  • Awareness needed across both existing users and net-new prospects

Operational

  • Launch infrastructure largely had to be built from scratch
  • No pre-existing landing page or product marketing template
  • Sales team needed enablement for a newly introduced category
  • Design and creative systems needed to extend for new module

04 — Product Marketing

Translating Technical Capability into Market Positioning

The core product marketing challenge was translating complex midstream data capabilities into clear, business-oriented value propositions that resonated with both technical users and commercial decision-makers.

Positioning Framework

  • As a platform extension — not a separate product, but a natural evolution of existing analytics
  • As a workflow enhancement — reducing fragmentation between upstream and midstream teams
  • As a differentiated capability — integrated research and facility intelligence in one place

Audience Translation

  • For technical users — precise facility data, network visualisation, volume analytics
  • For commercial users — operational efficiency, infrastructure visibility, single-platform workflow
  • For existing customers — platform expansion, no new tools, immediate workflow value
05 — Strategic Impact

Why This Launch Mattered

  1. 01

    Platform diversification beyond upstream analytics

    The Midstream Module repositioned the platform from a well-and-production analytics tool to a broader energy intelligence platform — materially expanding the total addressable market and strengthening the product narrative for prospects with cross-stream needs.

  2. 02

    Increased platform stickiness through workflow integration

    By enabling users to compare well data and facility data within a single platform, the module created cross-stream workflow dependency — making the platform significantly harder to replace and deepening engagement for customers with both upstream and midstream operations.

  3. 03

    New user segment unlocked — midstream operators and facility managers

    Existing platform users were predominantly upstream-focused. The Midstream Module opened conversations with a new class of users — facilities managers, infrastructure analysts, and midstream operators — who had not previously been addressable with the existing product.

  4. 04

    Stronger cross-functional value proposition for integrated accounts

    For accounts with both upstream production and midstream infrastructure, the integrated platform reduced the case for point solutions. A single platform covering both workflows became a compelling consolidation argument — improving competitive positioning in enterprise conversations.
