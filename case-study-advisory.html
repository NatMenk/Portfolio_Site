01 — Overview

Project Overview

At a global B2B advisory firm serving technical and commercial clients in the energy sector, I managed marketing operations, communications, events, and sales enablement infrastructure to support business development, brand visibility, and cross-regional campaign execution.

The role extended beyond campaign execution into operational ownership of the company's marketing systems, event programmes, external communications, and internal reporting processes — functioning as the operational bridge between marketing, business development, leadership, vendors, and external partners.

Operational Context

The business relied heavily on events and relationship-driven marketing, making operational precision critical. With multiple conferences, sponsorships, and proprietary events running annually across regions, consistent execution was the baseline expectation — not an occasional achievement.

02 — Event Management

End-to-End Event Operations

Events were the primary business development channel — and required a level of operational rigour that went well beyond logistics. I managed both external conference participation and proprietary company-hosted experiences, each with distinct requirements.

External

Conferences, Sponsorships and Trade Shows

Managed end-to-end sponsorship operations for major industry conferences — coordinating deliverables, serving as primary liaison with event organisers, submitting required assets, facilitating payment workflows, and ensuring deadline compliance. Created parallel LinkedIn campaigns to amplify company visibility around each conference, ensuring strong digital presence alongside physical attendance.

Proprietary

Executive Conferences and Client Networking Events

Managed operational execution for flagship company events including executive conferences, client networking functions, and relationship-building events. Built and managed the full digital infrastructure — email invitation campaigns, event landing pages, RSVP systems, attendee communications, and calendar integrations via Mailchimp and AddEvent. Oversaw venue coordination, catering, vendor communications, logistics, and invoice processing.

Onsite

Live Event Operations Lead

During live events, served as operational lead, reception coordinator, and guest access control — functioning as the single escalation point for onsite issues and emergency situations. This role required the operational systems to be sound enough to focus on real-time guest experience rather than logistics firefighting.

03 — Core Workstreams

Five Areas of Operational Ownership

Area 01
External Communications and Brand

Managed all external communications — client-facing content, event promotions, corporate announcements, and brand visibility content aligned to key industry moments. Produced and published LinkedIn posts, company announcements, and campaign messaging to ensure consistent brand representation across channels.

Channels managed
  • LinkedIn organic and campaign content
  • Client-facing event communications
  • Corporate announcements and press releases
  • Conference visibility content
Area 02
Marketing Analytics and Reporting

Introduced regular reporting to give leadership operational visibility into marketing output. Monitored corporate social performance and LinkedIn analytics, prepared monthly reporting summaries, and created executive-level summaries consolidating completed projects, current initiatives, and marketing performance indicators.

Reporting delivered
  • Monthly social and campaign performance summaries
  • Executive summaries of marketing deliverables
  • Ongoing initiative tracking and status reporting
  • Marketing performance indicators for leadership
Area 03
Sales Enablement and Collateral

Maintained and evolved all major sales collateral for domestic and international markets — including design refreshes, new page creation, and PandaDoc workflow management. Produced audience-specific presentations and translated materials into Spanish and Russian for international business development.

Materials managed
  • Domestic and international proposal materials
  • Master, international, and specialist service brochures
  • Corporate slide deck refreshes and custom presentations
  • Localised materials in English, Spanish, and Russian
Area 04
Brand Assets and Vendor Management

Owned branded merchandise procurement and the supporting vendor ecosystem — designing custom branded assets, managing apparel and conference materials, and conducting recurring inventory reviews to ensure promotional asset availability for events and team needs year-round.

Assets managed
  • Apparel, conference materials, branded giveaways
  • Business cards and printed collateral
  • Vendor relationships for design and production
  • Recurring inventory and availability tracking
Area 05 — Operational Documentation and Knowledge Transfer

To ensure continuity and reduce operational risk, created centralized documentation covering all marketing applications, platform ownership, vendor contacts, workflow references, and operational dependencies. Knowledge transfer materials spanned email platforms, RSVP systems, design platforms, external vendors, venue contacts, and creative resources — enabling smoother transition planning and reducing knowledge concentration risk.

04 — Business Impact

What Operational Ownership Enabled

  1. 01

    Reliable execution of major conferences and sponsorships

    With end-to-end ownership of sponsorship deliverables, asset submissions, and deadline management, the company maintained consistent, professional presence at industry events without missed commitments or last-minute scrambles — protecting brand credibility in a relationship-driven sector.

  2. 02

    Improved leadership visibility through monthly reporting

    Prior to structured reporting, marketing output was largely invisible to leadership outside of specific deliverables. Monthly executive summaries introduced systematic visibility into completed work, ongoing initiatives, and performance indicators — enabling more data-informed decision-making across the commercial function.

  3. 03

    International sales capability expanded through localised materials

    Translation and localisation of sales collateral into Spanish and Russian removed a friction point in international business development — ensuring commercial teams could engage prospects in key markets with professional, brand-consistent materials rather than ad hoc translated documents.

  4. 04

    Operational continuity protected through documentation

    Centralised documentation of all marketing systems, platforms, vendors, and workflows reduced knowledge concentration risk materially — ensuring that operational capability wasn't dependent on any single person's institutional memory and enabling smoother transition planning.

  5. 05

    Stronger digital and physical brand presence across channels

    By owning both the LinkedIn content calendar and the physical event presence — from branded merchandise to onsite operations — the company maintained consistent brand expression across every channel where clients and prospects encountered it.
